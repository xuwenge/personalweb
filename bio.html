About Me

Bio

I am a lecturer in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and an active researcher at the HCI group of the Digital Media Technology Lab. I specialise in HCI, Extended (Virtual, Augmented, Mixed) Reality (XR), User Experience, 3D User Interface, Serious Games.

I earned my PhD in Computer Science in 2021 with "Motion-based Interaction for Head-Mounted Displays” at the University of Liverpool. My PhD studies were supported by a Postgraduate Research Scholarship by Xi’an Jiaotong-Liverpool University (RDF 15-02-12). I am an award-winning academic and researcher. My awards include IEEE VR Best Conference Paper Nominee, ACM CHI PLAY Student Game Design Competition Finalist, Outstanding Teaching Team Award (undergraduate program) in Suzhou, China.

I have a strong HCI, XR, and Serious Games background. In the past four years, I has published roughly 30 International journals and conferences papers, including posters. Many of these publications are published in top-tier HCI/XR conferences like ACM CHI, IEEE VR, IEEE ISMAR; top-ranked journals on graphics and visualizations like IEEE TVCG and SIGGRAPH (i3D); and leading serious game journals like JMIR serious games and Games for Health. In addition, I actively acts as a reviewer for leading conferences (e.g., ACM CHI, IEEE VR, IEEE ISMAR) and journals (International Journal of Human-Computer Studies, JMIR serious games, IEEE Transactions on Games).
